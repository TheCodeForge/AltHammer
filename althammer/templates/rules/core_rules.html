Core Rules

# Introduction AltHammer is a turn-based activation tabletop wargaming experience. It is intended to be played with your existing collection of wargaming models. # General Rules ## Priority In the event of inconsistency between two sources of rules, use the rule that is found in the earliest location in this list: * Unit rules card, including rules associated with any +Keywords+ that the unit possesses. * Detachment Rules * Faction Rules * Core Rules # Definitions See [Definitions](/rules/definitions) # Order of Play ## Determine Points Limit Together with your opponent, agree on a points limit for the game of at least 500 points or higher. Suggested limits: * Small: 500 points * Medium: 1000 points * Large: 2000 points * Epic: 3000+ points Additionally, if both players agree to it, the [Sideboard](sideboard_option) optional rule may be enabled. ## Select Faction and Detachment Select your army Faction and Detachment. You do not have to reveal your selections to your opponent at this time, but if your opponent reveals their selections to you, consider reciprocating. ## Muster Army Assemble a collection of units in your chosen Faction, up to the agreed points limit. Requirements: * All units must have the Faction Keyword matching your chosen Faction. * All units must meet any requirements or restrictions established by your chosen Faction's rules or your chosen Detachment's rules. * You must include at least one +Character+ model. * You may not include more than one copy of any given +Epic Hero+ unit. * You may not include more than six copies of any given +Battleline+ unit. * You may not include more than three copies of any other unit Select one +Character+ or +Epic Hero+ Model as your army's Warlord. That model gains the +Warlord+ keyword. ## Determine Mission Determine the deployment zone layout and place objective markers. ## Set Up Battlefield Set up a tabletop battlefield according to the agreed upon points limit: * 500-1999 points: 44" x 60" * 2000+ points: 44" x 90" Add terrain features in a mutually agreeable manner. Overall terrain layout should be either reflectively or rotationally symmetric. Ensure that the classification of all terrain features is mutually understood and agreed upon. See [Terrain](/core_rules/terrain) ## Reveal Armies and Formations Reveal your chosen Faction, Detachment, and mustered units to the opposing player, if you have not already done so. Identify which Model is your +Warlord+. Identify any models with the Leader rule, and declare which unit they are attached to. If attached, the leading Model gain the +Leader+ keyword. Models in the unit it is attached to gain the +Bodyguard+ keyword. Identify any units to be placed in Reserves. Set them aside. ## Sideboard Option The Sideboard Option may be enabled by mutual agreement between players during the Determine Points Limit step. If any player declines the Sideboard Option, it is not enabled. If the Sideboard Option is enabled: * During the [Muster Army](#muster_army) step, assemble a collection of units up to 500 points *above* the agreed points limit instead. * After the [Reveal Armies and Formations](#reveal_armies_and_formations) step, Roll Off. Beginning with the player who lost the Roll Off, players alternate selecting and removing units from their mustered army, until their remaining army is at or below the agreed points limit. * Your +Warlord+ Model must remain in your army. ## Deploy Armies Roll Off. Beginning with the player to win the Roll Off, alternate placing one unit onto the battlefield within your deployment zone. Once one player has deployed all available units, the other player may deploy all of their remaining units at once. Units must be placed onto the battlefield in [Unit Coherency](/rules/definitions#unit_coherency). ## The Battle Round The battle consists of five Battle Rounds. Each Battle Round contains the following steps: * [Round Start](#round_start) * [Generate Command Points](#generate_command_points) * [Roll Battle-Shock](#roll_battle-shock) * [Roll Initiative](#roll_initiative) * [Deploy Reserves](#deploy_reserves) (Round 2+) * [Alternating Activation](#alternating_activation) * [Cleanup](#cleanup) * [Scoring](#scoring) * [Round End](#round_end) ### Round Start Any effects or Strategems that happen "at the start of the Battle Round" should be invoked here. ### Generate Command Points Both players recieve 1 Command Point. ### Roll Battle-Shock For each unit that is at or below Half Strength, roll 2d6. If the result is less than the greatest Leadership property in the unit, the unit is Battle-shocked until the end of the Battle Round. Battle-Shocked units: * Cannot be selected by Strategems. * Have an Objective Control property of 0 * Cannot Move (or Advance) out of Melee Distance. ### Roll Initiative Each player rolls 2d6 and subtracts the Leadership property of their +Warlord+. The player with the greater result wins the Initiative roll. In the event of a tie, both players re-roll. ### Deploy Reserves This phase is skipped during the first Battle Round. Beginning with the player that won the Initiative roll, alternate deploying units in Reserves to the Battlefield, if so desired. Units must be placed at least 9" away from all enemy units. Units may be placed into different areas of the battlefield, depending on the round: * Round 2: Your deployment zone * Round 3: Your deployment zone, the neutral zone * Round 4: Your deployment zone, the neutral zone, anywhere within 6" of the Battlefield edge * Round 5: Anywhere Units must be placed onto the battlefield in [Unit Coherency](/rules/definitions#unit_coherency). ### Alternating Activation Beginning with the player that won the Initiative roll, alternate selecting a unit to Activate. Units may not be Activated more than once in the Battle Round. Once one player has Activated all available units, the other player may Activate all of their remaining units. See the Activation section below. ### Cleanup Remove all dead models from the Battlefield. The models in any unit with a removed dead model may move 2" to maintain Unit Coherency. ### Scoring Each player scores Victory Points based on mission rules and the state of the Battlefield. ### Round End Any effects which last "until the end of the Battle Round" end here. Any effects or Strategems which take place "at the end of the Battle Round" should be invoked here. # Activation When Activated, a unit may do up to two of the following actions: * [Move](#move) * [Advance](#advance) * [Magik](#magik) (+Psyker+ units only) * [Shoot](#shoot) * [Charge](#charge) * [Fight](#fight) ## Move Each Model in the unit may move up to the number of inches indicated by its Movement property. Models may use their Movement to move horizontally, or vertically up or down terrain. Models with +Fly+ may move diagonally through the air to go over terrain. Models with +Fortification+ may not move at all. ### Restrictions The following restrictions apply to any form of movement, whether performed as a part of a Move action or for any other reason: * Units in Melee Distance of an enemy unit may not move through enemy Models. * Models may not end their movement on another Model or its base * Models may not move through enemy Models. * Models may not end a movement within Melee Distance of enemy Models. * Models may not end their movement partially up a wall or other terrain obstacle. * Units must end their movement in [Unit Coherency](/rules/definitions#unit_coherency). ### Unit Coherency At any point in the game (except during a Unit's movement), if a Unit is found to *not* be in [Unit Coherency](/rules/definitions#unit_coherency), the player controlling that unit must immediately remove Models from the Unit until Unit Coherency is achieved. When removing Models to achieve Unit Coherency, do not activate On Death rules for the removed Models. ## Advance Must be paired with a Move action in the same Activation. Roll d6; Move the unit up to that many inches towards the opponent's edge of the battlefield, or towards the nearest enemy unit, as described in the Move action above. ## Magik Only +Psyker+ Models may perform this action. Select a spell from your Detachment rules. For each +Psyker+ Model in the unit, make a Psychic test: Roll 2d6 and add 1 to the result for each of the following keywords the Model has: * +Character+ * +Warlord+ * +Epic Hero+ If the result of the Psychic test is greater than or equal to the spell's Difficulty (shown in brackets next to the spell name), then the spell is successful. Resolve the effects of the spell. ### Perils of Power If the result of the Psychic test was an unmodified 2 or unmodified 12, then the Model suffers Perils of Power: The Model's unit suffers d3 Mortal Damage. (For an unmodified 12, the spell is still successful; resolve the spell's effects first.) ### Deny the Psychic If the Psychic test is passed, the opposing player may attempt to Deny the Psychic before the spell effects are resolved. The opposing player selects a +Psyker+ Model within 18" of the Activated +Psyker+ Model, and makes a Psychic test. If the result of the opposing Psychic test is greater than the Activated Model's Psychic test, then the spell is blocked and does not take effect. ### Smite All +Psyker+ Models know the spell Smite: > **[6] Smite** > > Select a target within 18". It suffers d6 Mortal Damage. Until the end of the Battle Round, increase the Difficulty of this spell by 1. ## Shoot Select a target enemy unit. For each Model in your unit with line-of-sight to at least one enemy unit Model, select a Ranged weapon profile to attack with; see the Combat section below. ## Charge Select a target enemy unit. Roll 2d6 for Charge Movement; if the result is greater than or equal to the distance to the enemy unit (inches, rounded down), move the unit directly towards the enemy unit until they are in Melee Distance. No Model may move a greater distance than the Charge Movement result. +Aircraft+ cannot be selected as a Charge target unless all models in the charging unit have +Fly+. After a Charge, both the unit and its target enemy unit shall perform a Fight action, selecting the other unit as the target. ## Fight Select a target enemy unit that is within Melee Distance. Choose a melee weapon profile to attack with; see the Combat section below. +Aircraft+ cannot be selected as a Fight target unless all models in the attacking unit have +Fly+. # Combat All combat is made with a weapon profile listed on the attacking unit's data card. The attacking model makes a number of attacks equal to the Attacks property of the weapon profile. For each attack: * Attacker rolls to Hit. If successful, continue. * Attacker rolls to Wound. If successful, continue. * Defender rolls to Save. If failed, damage is applied to the target. ## Hit Roll Attacker rolls d6. If the result is greater than or equal to the Skill property of the weapon profile, the attack hits and the attack sequence continues. Otherwise, the attack misses, and the attack sequence ends here. Unmodified Hit Rolls of 1 are treated as "Critical Fails", and are always unsuccessful, regardless of any modifiers. Unmodified Hit Rolls of 6 are treated as "Critical Hits", and are always successful, regardless of any modifiers. ## Wound Roll Attacker rolls d6. Compare the attacking weapon profile's Strength property with the targeted unit's Toughness property to determine the required dice roll. If successfull, the attack hits solidly, and the attack sequence continues. Otherwise, the attack glances off, and the attack sequence ends here. * Strength half of Toughness or less: 6+ required * Strength less than Toughness: 5+ required * Strength equal to Toughness: 4+ required * Strength greater than Toughness: 3+ required * Strength double Toughness or greater: 2+ required. Unmodified Wound Rolls of 1 are treated as "Critical Fails", and are always unsuccessful, regardless of any modifiers. Unmodified Wound Rolls of 6 are treated as "Critical Wounds", and are always successful, regardless of any modifiers. ## Saving Throw The defending player selects a Model in the targeted unit that is within line of sight and range to the attacking unit. If a wounded Model is a legal option, select that Model. A +Leader+ Model must not be chosen, unless the attacking weapon is +[Precise]+ The defending player rolls d6 and worsens the result by the attacking weapon's Armor Penetration property. If the result is greater than or equal to the Armor Save property of the selected Model, then the target's armor absorbs the attack without consequence. Otherwise, the target Model is injured for a number of hitpoints equal to the Damage property of the attacking weapon. An unmodified roll of 1 is always a failed save. ### Invulnerable Save If the target Model has an Invulnerable Save property, the Armor Save cannot be modified to be worse than the Invulnerable Save. ### Feel No Pain If the target Model has a Feel No Pain rule property, after failing a Saving Throw, roll one d6 for each point of damage to be taken. If the result is greater than or equal to the Feel No Pain rule property, reduce the damage of the attack by 1. ## Apply Damage Apply the resulting damage to the Model selected in the Saving Throw step. If the Model is now at zero (or less) Health, the Model is now Dead. Tip the Model over onto its side (or use a token marker) to indicate that it is Dead, but do not remove it from the Battlefield yet. Narratively, all Activations occur at about the same moment of time, so the Model can still move, shoot, cast magik, charge and fight until the end of the Battle Round. ## Weapon Keywords See [Weapon Keywords](/rules/weapon_keywords) for a reference of keywords that modify the combat process.
